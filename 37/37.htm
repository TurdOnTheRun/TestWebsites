Did You Know?

  • The feeding habits of the T. rex and Mosasaurus are on display every 2 hours at the feeding shows.

  • The Apatosaurus reaches full size in only 10 years. Talk about a growth spurt!

  • The Gallimimus or “chicken mimic” weighs about 493 pounds224 kg more than its feathered namesake.

  • Feeding time! Get a close up view of prehistoric ocean life at the underwater observatory.

  • Nearly everything in the botanical garden is food to at least one of our dinosaurs.

  • T. rex eats up to 22 tons20 metric tons of meat a year… that’s the equivalent of 18 cows.

  • Do not tap on the glass, cross barriers or call out to the dinos. Your safety is our first priority.

  • You can see everything from Gallimimus Valley to the golf course from the top of the gondola lift.

  • You can see over 100 species of prehistoric life when you paddle out on the Cretaceous Cruise.

  • Triceratops love selfies. Stop by the petting zoo for your photo opportunity.

  • Triceratops love getting scratched behind their shield-like frill.

  • Dinosaurs are both hot- and cold-blooded. Usually carnivores are hot; herbivores run cold.

  • It would take a raptor 20 minutes to run across Isla Nublar at its widest.

  • A newborn human baby has a bigger brain than most fully grown dinosaurs.

  • The T. rex’s widely mocked tiny arms are actually incredibly powerful.

  • The Stegosaurus’ brain is about the size of a walnut.

  • The plates on a Stegosaurus’ back can grow up to 30 inches76 cm. That’s about the length of a skateboard.

  • You can refill any souvenir mug for just 99 cents at any kiosk in the park.

  • Some dinosaurs’ tails are as long as a city bus, stretching to over 45 feet14 meters.

  • The feeding shows are not for the faint of heart and may be disturbing for small children.

  • If you lined up each piece of Mike and Ike® candy made each year, they would reach around the Earth 3 times.
